BIO

Nikos Voyiatzis is an artist, archivist, and educator whose work explores the intersections of digital culture, networked media, and archival practices. His projects often examine how information is organized and preserved in online environments, focusing on the political and aesthetic dimensions of digital archiving. Voyiatzis has been involved in various initiatives that highlight the role of user-generated content and digital folklore in contemporary culture. He has contributed to the preservation and documentation of audiovisual, digital and online art and archives. His work often addresses themes such as the informations organisation, the impact of algorithms on information dissemination, and the cultural significance of digital and online artifacts.

Voyiatzis is active in education, designing workshops and courses that encourage critical engagement with digital media. He has developed educational materials on topics like digital creativity, information literacy, and the preservation of cultural heritage in the digital age. His teaching emphasizes the importance of understanding digital tools and platforms as both creative mediums and subjects of critical inquiry. Voyiatzis's work has been featured in various exhibitions and festivals.
